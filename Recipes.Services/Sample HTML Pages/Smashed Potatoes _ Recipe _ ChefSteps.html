Smashed Potatoes

  • Pinterest
  • Google+
  • Embed code

They say pizza is like sex: even when it’s bad, it’s pretty good. That’s actually not true of either of those things, but it is kind of true of mashed potatoes. Okay, you have to manage to cook the potatoes and get them to a creamy, purée-esque consistency, but if you can pull that off, you’re good to go.

That said, it’s way easy to improve on this humble side dish. Use flavorful fingerling potatoes, for instance. Add good cream and butter, plus some chives for bite and color, and you’ve elevated an everyday staple into something decadent and delicious.

And that’s exactly what we’ve done here. We give our spuds a thorough scrubbing and leave the skins on for a sort of relaxed, weeknight version of Pomme Purée. It’s the one to make when you’ve had a long, frustrating day at work and you need something comforting to partner with a juicy steak, gravy-dressed turkey breast, or crisp-skinned roasted chicken. Add a generous pour of food-friendly wine, and you’ve got yourself a restorative meal that—for most of us—is way sexier than the stuff they’re slinging at the neighborhood pizza place.*

*Caveat: This may not apply if your neighborhood is located in New York City or Philadelphia, certain areas of New Jersey and Connecticut, or most anywhere in Italy.

Clean potatoes thoroughly.

Drain water from the pot and add in cream.

Lightly mash the potatoes with a fork. Add butter and fold into mashed potatoes until fully incorporated. Season with salt and pepper.

Cover to keep warm.
